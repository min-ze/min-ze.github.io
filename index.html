Life in Junior College

Looking back, 2 years of JC went by very quickly. Those 2 years were packed with many
memorable experiences and I was fortunate to be a part of the Student Council which gave me
even more opportunities to learn and grow. The numerous hours spent planning for events,
working on proposals and conducting debriefing sessions has undoubtedly honed my critical thinking
and public speaking skills tremendously.

One of my favourite memories in Junior College include being part of the planning
committee for the 2018 freshman orientation. It was definitely challening both in
the planning and execution phase since the entire event involved more than a
thousand students and staffs alike. However, with everyone's effort, the
orientation was a successful one with the freshmen enjoying the 7-day long
orientation that took 3 months to plan and prepare. It definitely felt rewarding
that I was able to give the freshmen a better or similar experience to that of my
own when I was a freshmen myself.

Another unforgettable experience would be my overseas exchange trip to Chongqing,
China. Together with my fellow student councilors, we were able to meet and
interact with local students from one of the renowned schools in the region.
It was a fruitful and eye-opening trip as we got to experience their education
system and learn more about their culture and way of life. Overall, it was a
meaningful trip and a much needed getaway.
